Production

Design Elements

  • Red (chili red) gives a deep and strong vibe. It can be associated with passion, action and courage. It is a very vibrant color and can be aggressive too.
  • Black (eerie black) is a powerful color and gives a mysterious vibe.
  • Purple, and especially periwinkle which combines blue and violet, motivates and is a positive and upbeat color which perfectly complements the other colors in this palette for the rock vibe.
  • Yellow (xanthous) is bright and cheerful which can be associated with the happy and great atmosphere of events and rock. It is an energizing color.
  • Grey (dim grey) is timeless and neutral and gives some calmness in the color palette. It has a damping effect on the mood and is associated with isolation and neutrality. It brings coolness and balance.
  • Logo choices
      • Our logo originated from the idea of combining hard rock and road trippers. The guitar represents hard rock, and instead of strings, it is depicted as a highway, expressing the brand name "rock trippers."
  • Font choices
      • The Superclarendon Black italic font is carefully chosen with our target audience in mind. The font has a strong and bold look which fits our brand identity and sets the tone and mood of our website. The font is memorable because of the italics, and it has an old style.
        Oswald Outfit has a stong feel to it, but is not overpowering. The simple pattern resembles the accountability of our company. It is brave, but not too much.
      • User interface patterns and the structure of the navigation and content
        • On the About Page, users can easily plan their event because of the Plan Now button. In this call-to-action part, they can also see the biggest event that there currently is for them next to the planning part. Beneath the call-to-action part, users can see our vision and purpose of the site. Underneath this our values can be seen which immediately shows our position and purpose. When clicking on the Plan Now! Button, users will be redirected to the products our site offers. The products are sorted in cards and users immediately have a clear overview of the products we offer and the choices they can make. A short and clear description is given about the package they can choose. Additionally, users can navigate to the products through the navigation bar. Users can quickly go to our social media pages through the social media buttons at the bottom of the pages. With our strong statements such as: 'festivals like never before' and 'get together, rock it' we give the impression of an unique experience like never before. We offer All-Inclusive trips to the events which is our unique value proposal of our product.
        • The design of the website is clear and easy to navigate through. Buttons quickly show what can be done on the page and what the purpose is on the page. The main purpose of the website is for users to quickly find information about big events and to plan their trip. Our tone of voice is direct, casual and enthusiastic and this fits our audience because Hardrock fans coming to our website want to know about events and like that atmosphere. They are the type of person to go to festivals, enjoy their time and wanting to have no worries and convenience. Our website quickly shows users what they can do and what we offer to them. Users can see that they can plan their event and that we offer them an All-Inclusive experience which is our unique value proposal.

      Testing Report

      • We want to test the website with our target audience to understand how they navigate through the website. We want to track how easy and convenient it is for them to plan a fully planned event and track how they find information.
      • We aim to test the website with three participants from our target audience. Testing will be done on a desktop and in the mobile version as well. The testing can be executed in any setting.

        The instructions that will be given to the participants are: plan an All-Inclusive event, what is the main event of the year, what is the difference between packages, what is the purpose of the website and the service? The testing will be filmed as evidence.
    • Testing results:
      • A positive finding was that the user completed all of the tasks succesfully. They understood the purpose and could find the information quickly.
      • A negative finding was that the website was not a fully functional website yet. Accordingly, the price button did not direct to any further page.
      • 5 E's of usability testing:
      • Effective: the user executed all of the tasks succesfully, so that goal has been accomplished.
      • Efficient: the user executed the tasks quite easily, however not all of the buttons are fully functional yet.
      • Engaging: the user had a clear overview of the packages and the navigation was easy as well
      • Error tolerant: the user could navigate back and forth through the navigation bar and this was easy for them.
      • Easy to learn: the interface and design was clear for the user. Just as our goal was for the website: ease and convenience
      • Improvements
        • for the website will be: fully functional price buttons that will redirect the user to a new page with detailed explanations about the packages. In the new page user can customize their choices and they will be further informed about the price of each element. Further improvements would be a fully functional website and design elements such as fonts, colors and structures could be implemented in a more professional way.

Niche groups like as described are often very loyal customers when they have found a brand or product which they enjoy, since there are not many other brands or products just like it. We want to make use of this because just like our brand, there are not many competitors who have the same to offer as us. The main platforms which this target group uses are Instagram and Facebook.     Validation of Assumptions

    Our logo tells the story of our brand's essence, born from the fusion of two vital components: hard rock and road trippers. The term "road trippers" encapsulates the essence of travel, a fundamental part of who we are. At the heart of our logo lies a guitar, symbolizing the raw power and spirit of hard rock music. However, instead of the conventional strings, we've transformed the guitar into a highway, a direct nod to our brand identity, "rock trippers.”

    This deliberate choice embodies the travel experience we provide our customers: a seamless journey along the path of hard rock music, where every twist and turn is an adventure waiting to be discovered. It's about embracing the freedom of the open road, the thrill of exploration, and the camaraderie of fellow enthusiasts.

    By reimagining the guitar as a highway, we've crafted a visual representation of our brand's commitment to offering a carefree travel experience, where the road itself becomes a stage for unforgettable memories and exhilarating encounters.

    As our journey progressed, we uncovered an unexpected rival in Festival Travel. However, we quickly realized that while they focus on large-scale festivals, our niche lies in smaller, more authentic hard rock events. Furthermore, our website's design speaks directly to hard rock fans, with its edgy aesthetic and intuitive interface. It's a platform designed to ignite the passion of music lovers and inspire them to embark on their next adventure with us.

    Essentially, our logo shows what we're all about and attracts people who love hard rock and traveling. It's a sign of our strong commitment to giving people an amazing travel experience, step by step.
